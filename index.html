    How it works

    Neural networks, in your browser

    V-Image runs EfficientNet-Lite0 directly in your browser tab using WebAssembly & GPU acceleration. No data ever leaves your device.

    01

    Model loads

    EfficientNet-Lite0 (~25 MB) is fetched from Google's CDN and cached in your browser. On repeat visits it loads in under a second.

    02

    Image is pre-processed

    Your image is resized and normalized for the model input. Nothing is sent to any server.

    03

    Inference runs

    MediaPipe runs the model via WebAssembly with GPU delegate acceleration — inference completes in milliseconds.

    04

    Top predictions shown

    The top 5 classes by confidence are ranked and displayed with percentage scores and animated confidence bars.

    FAQ

    Common questions

    Is my image uploaded anywhere?

    No. Your image never leaves your device. V-Image loads the neural network model once from a CDN, then all processing happens locally inside your browser tab. We have no server that receives image data.

    Why does it take a few seconds to start?

    The first time you visit, your browser downloads EfficientNet-Lite0 (~25 MB) from Google's CDN. After that it is cached and loads instantly on return visits.

    How accurate is it?

    EfficientNet-Lite0 achieves around 80% top-1 accuracy on the ImageNet benchmark — better than MobileNet's 75%. It works best on clear, well-lit photos of single objects.

    What kinds of objects can it recognise?

    The model was trained on ImageNet's 1,000 classes which include animals, vehicles, food, household objects, plants, musical instruments, clothing, and more. It does not recognise faces, text, or document content.

    Can I use this on mobile?

    Yes. V-Image is fully responsive. On mobile, tap "Browse files" to select from your photo library or camera roll. Inference may be slightly slower on older devices.
