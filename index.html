    How it works

    Neural networks, in your browser

    V-Image runs a full convolutional neural network directly in your browser tab using WebGL acceleration. No data ever leaves your device.

    01

    Model loads

    MobileNet v3 (~16 MB) is fetched from CDN and cached in your browser. On repeat visits it loads in under a second.

    02

    Image is pre-processed

    Your image is resized to 224×224 pixels and normalized into a float tensor. Nothing is sent to any server.

    03

    Inference runs

    TensorFlow.js pushes the tensor through 90+ neural network layers via WebGL GPU acceleration in milliseconds.

    04

    Top predictions shown

    The top 5 classes by confidence are ranked and displayed with percentage scores and animated confidence bars.

    FAQ

    Common questions

    Is my image uploaded anywhere?

    No. Your image never leaves your device. V-Image loads the neural network model once from a CDN, then all processing happens locally inside your browser tab using JavaScript and WebGL. We have no server that receives image data.

    Why does it take a few seconds to start?

    The first time you visit, your browser needs to download the MobileNet model (~16 MB) from a CDN. After that it is cached and loads instantly on return visits. The status badge in the top-right corner shows live loading progress.

    How accurate is it?

    MobileNet v3 achieves around 75% top-1 accuracy on the ImageNet benchmark. It works best on clear, well-lit photos of single objects. Complex scenes or unusual angles will produce lower confidence scores — check the percentages to gauge certainty.

    What kinds of objects can it recognise?

    The model was trained on ImageNet's 1,000 classes which include animals, vehicles, food, household objects, plants, musical instruments, clothing, and more. It does not recognise faces, text, or document content.

    Can I use this on mobile?

    Yes. V-Image is fully responsive. On mobile, tap "Browse files" to select from your photo library or camera roll. Inference may be slightly slower on older devices due to limited GPU capability.
